BUREAU OF LAND MANAGEMENT — BATTLE MOUNTAIN DISTRICT, NEVADA
REQUEST FOR PROPOSALS
Solicitation No. BLM-NV-2026-0042

TITLE: Silver Creek Abandoned Mine Land Reclamation Project

1. PROJECT BACKGROUND
The Bureau of Land Management (BLM) Battle Mountain District Office seeks qualified contractors to perform mine land reclamation on approximately 200 acres of abandoned silver mining operations located in the Toiyabe Range, Lander County, Nevada. The Silver Creek Mine operated from 1878 to 1957 and has left significant disturbance including open adits, waste rock piles, tailings impoundments, and acid mine drainage affecting Silver Creek, a tributary to the Reese River.

The site is located within the Great Basin ecoregion at elevations of 6,200–6,800 feet. Surrounding undisturbed vegetation consists of Wyoming big sagebrush, bitterbrush, bluebunch wheatgrass, Indian ricegrass, and Sandberg bluegrass communities. Greater sage-grouse have been documented within 2 miles of the project area.

2. SCOPE OF WORK
The contractor shall provide all labor, materials, equipment, and supervision necessary to:
- Conduct a comprehensive site assessment including soil, water, and vegetation baseline surveys
- Remove or stabilize hazardous mine features (adits, shafts, unstable waste rock)
- Treat acid mine drainage sources and install passive treatment systems
- Reconstruct soils on disturbed areas using suitable growth media
- Revegetate all disturbed areas with locally-sourced native plant species appropriate to the Great Basin sagebrush-steppe ecosystem
- Implement erosion control measures throughout the project
- Conduct a minimum 5-year post-implementation monitoring program
- Develop and execute an adaptive management plan

3. EVALUATION CRITERIA
Proposals will be evaluated on a best-value basis using the following factors:

Factor 1: Technical Approach (40%)
- Restoration methodology and ecological soundness
- Native seed and plant material plan
- Monitoring and adaptive management approach

Factor 2: Past Performance (25%)
- Demonstrated experience on similar mine reclamation projects
- Documented restoration success rates

Factor 3: Staffing and Qualifications (20%)
- Key personnel qualifications (restoration ecologist, PM, hydrologist)
- Relevant certifications

Factor 4: Cost (15%)
- Reasonableness and realism of proposed costs

4. PERIOD OF PERFORMANCE
Base period: 24 months for site preparation and implementation
Option Year 1-3: Annual monitoring, reporting, and adaptive management
Option Year 4-5: Final monitoring and closeout

5. SUBMISSION REQUIREMENTS
Contractors shall submit a detailed Statement of Work (SOW) addressing each evaluation factor. Proposals are due no later than 4:00 PM PT, June 15, 2026.
