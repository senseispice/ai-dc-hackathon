STATEMENT OF WORK
Western Environmental Services Inc.
Silver Creek Abandoned Mine Land Reclamation Project
BLM-NV-2026-0042

1. COMPANY OVERVIEW
Western Environmental Services Inc. (WES) is a full-service environmental consulting and remediation firm headquartered in Salt Lake City, Utah with regional offices across the Intermountain West. WES employs approximately 200 professionals and has completed over 30 environmental remediation and restoration projects for federal and state agencies since 2001.

2. TECHNICAL APPROACH

2.1 Site Assessment
WES will perform site characterization including soil sampling, surface water quality monitoring, and a vegetation inventory. We will collect soil samples at representative locations to characterize contamination levels and determine soil amendment needs. Water quality will be monitored at key points along Silver Creek.

2.2 Mine Feature Stabilization
We will close dangerous mine openings using appropriate closure methods and regrade waste rock piles to stable configurations. Acid mine drainage will be addressed through installation of a treatment system designed to raise pH and reduce dissolved metals.

2.3 Soil Preparation and Amendment
Disturbed areas will receive soil amendments as needed to support plant growth. We will apply topsoil where available and add organic amendments and lime to adjust pH on acid-generating substrates. All areas will be ripped to reduce compaction before planting.

2.4 Revegetation
We will develop a native seed mix appropriate for the site's elevation and ecological setting. The mix will include native grasses, forbs, and shrubs common to the Great Basin region. Some seed may be sourced from regional suppliers in Nevada, Utah, and Oregon to ensure adequate supply. Shrubs including sagebrush and bitterbrush will be planted as container stock.

Seeding will be performed using a rangeland drill where terrain allows, and broadcast seeding on steeper slopes. We will target fall planting windows for optimal germination.

2.5 Erosion Control
Standard erosion BMPs will be installed including silt fencing, straw wattles, and mulching on disturbed slopes. Check dams will be installed in drainage features as needed.

3. MONITORING PLAN
WES will conduct annual monitoring for 3 years following implementation, with an option to extend to 5 years. Monitoring will include:
- Visual assessment of revegetation progress
- Photo documentation at established photo points
- General vegetation cover estimates
- Water quality sampling at selected stations
- Summary annual reports to BLM

We will adapt our approach if monitoring indicates revegetation is not progressing as expected.
